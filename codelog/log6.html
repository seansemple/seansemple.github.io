CSS Color – Media Query Research

Q1) What did you look up?


I chose CSS media queries.

Q2) What sites did you consult? Include links to the specific pages on those 4 resources.

  • Stackdiary – CSS Media Queries
  • MDN – Media Queries
  • W3Schools – CSS3 Media Queries
  • CSS-Tricks – Complete Guide to Media Queries

Q3) What are the differences in the information presented on each site?

Stackdiary gives the broadest, most detailed information on media features.
MDN is the best at teaching concepts and application, whereas W3Schools is the simplest and sort of like a cheat sheet.
Lastly, CSS-Tricks focuses on practical application and real-world uses for media queries.

Q4) What features do you think a code reference page should offer?

Clear definitions and real world applications are really important.
I also think good word economy is important in technical writing. Additionally, appropriate examples are important.

Q5) Do any of your 4 resources stand out as particularly good or bad?

I think they're all pretty universally good. W3Schools doesn't go too deep into application.
StackDiary is the strongest overall as it feels the most up to date and well written.
