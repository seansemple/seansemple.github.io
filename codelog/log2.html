Absolute units are lengths that are fixed and do not change depending on other elements. Examples include: cm, mm, in, pt, and px. These represent physical lengths and are useful especially for print or precise layout.

Relative units depend on something else: they’re calculated relative to a reference, making layouts adaptable for various screens. Some examples: em, rem, vw, vh, %.

px - absolute; 1px = 1/96th of 1in

pt - absolute; 1pt = 1/72nd of 1in

em - relative; depends on the element’s font size

rem - relative; depends on the root element’s font size

vw - relative; depends on the viewport’s width

vh - relative; depends on the viewport’s height
