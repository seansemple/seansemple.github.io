Dill Hummus

Delicious classic hummus with a pickley bite.

Dill Hummus Ingredients

  • 1-2 cloves fresh garlic, sliced
  • juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 14-ounce can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ¼ cup tahini
  • 2 tablespoon fresh dill
  • salt and pepper, to taste
  • ¼ cup iced water

Directions

  1. Soften the garlic. Add the sliced garlic to a food processor bowl with the lemon juice. Let it sit and infuse while you cook the chickpeas.
  2. Cook the chickpeas. Put chickpeas and baking soda in a small pot, cover with water, bring to a boil, then simmer for about 12 minutes.
    This helps tenderize them and loosens the skins. Skim off any skins that float to the top as they cook.
  3. Drain and de-skin. When the chickpeas look a bit mushy (that’s good), drain and rinse under cool water. Pick off and discard any remaining
    skins you spot—no need to be perfect.
  4. Blend it all. Add everything to a food processor or high-speed blender and run it for about 1 minute. With the machine running, slowly stream
    in the iced water and blend up to another minute until ultra-smooth. Taste and adjust salt.
  5. Serve. Spread the hummus onto a platter, swirl the top with the back of a spoon, and let olive oil pool in the grooves. Finish with extra
    dill and freshly cracked black pepper.
